Music Theory: Modes?

Learn about the theory behind musical modes and investigate the colour they add to diatonic scales


What are modes?

In Western music, modes are simply scales with variations in their note intervals when compared to the major scale we are familiar with. In fact, Ionian mode, the 1st mode of the major scale, is actually just another name for the major scale (Aeolian mode is simply another name for the natural minor scale). There are seven modes of the major scale: Ionian, Dorian, Phrygian, Lydian, Mixolydian, Aeolian, and Locrian.

All of these modes can be derived from the major scale which has the following interval pattern: TONE, TONE, Semitone ,TONE, TONE, TONE, Semitone.
For exmple, starting with C the notes are C--D--E-F--G--A--B-C. This is called the Ionian mode. The 2nd mode, Dorian takes the first interval and moves it to the end thereby shifting all the other intervals by one place, giving us: TONE, Semitone ,TONE, TONE, TONE, Semitone, TONE. Starting with D the notes are D--E-F--G--A--B-C--D (the same notes as C Major but the order of intervals is different). But if for a better comparison we start on a C, the notes are C--D-E♭--F--G--A--B♭-C which is like a C minor scale with a sharpened 6th (A instead of A♭) which is what gives the Dorian mode its unique character.
