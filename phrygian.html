Phrygian Mode

The Phrygian Mode is the 3rd mode of the major scale so it starts and ends on the 3rd degree of that scale. If we play the notes of the C major scale but start on E we get the following intervals:
Semitone, TONE, TONE, TONE, Semitone, TONE, TONE.

This means E Phrygian is: E, F, G, A, B, C, D, E.

The Phrygian mode is often written as 1 ♭2 ♭3 4 5 ♭6 ♭7 and is similar to a minor scale but with a flatted 2nd.

E Major: E, F♯, G♯, A, B, C♯, D♯, E
E Minor: E, F♯, G, A, B, C, D, E
E Phrygian: E, F, G, A, B, C, D, E

The Phrygian and Aeolian(minor) modes are similar but the key difference is the b2 that makes it unusual and gives the mode a Spanish Flamenco quality. Examples of songs using the Phrygian mode are ‘Pyramid Song’ by Radiohead and ‘White Rabbit’ by Jefferson Airplane.
