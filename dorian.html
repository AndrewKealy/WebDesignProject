Dorian Mode

The Dorian Mode is the 2nd mode of the major scale so it starts and ends on the 2nd degree of that scale. If we play the notes of C major but starting on D we get the following intervals:
TONE, Semitone, TONE, TONE, TONE, Semitone, TONE.

This means D Dorian is: D, E, F, G, A, B, C, D.

This Dorian mode is often written as: 1 2 ♭3 4 5 6 ♭7 and can be considered to be a minor scale with a raised 6th.

D Major: D, E, F♯, G, A, B, C♯, D
D Minor: D, E, F, G, A, B♭, C, D
D Dorian: D, E, F, G, A, B, C, D

Dorian can be thought of as a “soulful” mode because of its minor, melancholic feel. The raised 6th, however, gives it a mystical character. Some noted examples of its use are in Pink Floyd’s ‘Another Brick in the Wall (Part II)’, The Beatles’ ‘Eleanor Rigby’ and the English folk song ‘Scarborough Fair’.
