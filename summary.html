Summary

Ionian The major scale, with a happy character, can be heard in Beethoven's appropriately titled "Ode to Joy"
Dorian The "soulful" mode, lending its minor feel to songs such as "Eleanor Rigby"
Phrygian Differentiated from Aeolian by b2, Phrygian's Flamenco quality has been has been adapted by Jefferson Airplane for "White Rabbit"
Lydian John Williams used Lydian's uplifting characteristics for the soaring "Flying Theme" in E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial
Mixolydian Angus Young owes his dirty blues sound in "Back in Black" to the the flatted 7th of the Mixolydian mode
Aeolian Its sad feel make Aeolian the go to mode for musical contemplation, as with REM's "Losing My Religion"
Locrian Possibly too sinister for contemporary pop, the Locrian mode can be found lurking in Debussy’s ‘Jeux’
