Ionian Mode (Major scale)

Whether you know it or not, if you play any music at all, you are familiar with the Ionian Mode - it is just another name for the Major scale. So, for example, the D Major scale contains the exact same notes and intervals as the D Ionian Mode, the E Major scale contains the same notes as the E Ionian Mode and so on.

The intervals or distances between each note are as follows:
TONE, TONE, Semitone,TONE, TONE, TONE, Semitone.

Therefore C Ionian is: C, D, E, F, G, A, B, C.

The Ionian mode has a happy character, think 'Twinkle Twinkle Little Star'. Ionian melodies work over song with the ubiquitous I - IV - V chord progression. Some examples of Ionian mode in classical and modern music are 'Ode To Joy' by Beethoven, 'Let It Be' by The Beatles and 'Brown-Eyed Girl' by Van Morrison.
