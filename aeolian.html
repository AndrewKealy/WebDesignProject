Aeolian Mode (Minor scale)

The Aeolian Mode is the 6th mode of the major scale so it starts and ends on the 6th degree of that scale. If we play the notes of the C major scale but start on A we get the following intervals:
TONE, Semitone, TONE, TONE, Semitone, TONE, TONE.

This means A Aeolian is: A, B, C, D, E, F, G, A.

The Aeolian mode can be written as 1 2 ♭3 4 5 ♭6 ♭7 and is therefore simply the natural minor scale and is the relative minor to C major.

A Major: A, B, C♯, D, E, F♯, G♯, A
A Aeolian: A, B, C, D, E, F, G, A

The Aeolian mode has a sad, contemplative feel and is very common in both modern and classical music. Examples of Aeolian song structure are ‘All Along the Watchtower’ by Bob Dylan, ‘Losing My Religion’ by R.E.M. and Jimmy Page’s solo in ‘Stairway to Heaven’.
