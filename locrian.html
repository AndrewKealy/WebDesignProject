Locrian Mode

The Locrian Mode is the 7th mode of the major scale so it starts and ends on the 7th degree of that scale. If we play the notes of the C major scale but start on B we get the following intervals:
Semitone, TONE, TONE, Semitone, TONE, TONE, TONE.

This means B Locrian is: B, C, D, E, F, G, A, B.

The Locrian mode is often written as 1 ♭2 ♭3 4 ♭5 ♭6 ♭7 and can be described as being neither major nor minor – it has the unusual sounds of the ♭2 and the ♭5.

B Major: G♯ A♯ B C♯ D♯ E F♯ G
B Minor: B, C♯, D, E, F♯, G, A, B
B Locrian: B, C, D, E, F, G, A, B

The Locrian mode has a very sinister sound due to the presence of the dissonant b5 (tritone) interval. As a result it very uncommon to hear a whole song based in the Locrian mode and would be more suited to giving a dark feel to a certain part of a song or a turnaround. It is difficult to find very recognisable examples of the Locrian mode, although the bassline in Bjorks ‘Army of Me’ is close. A classical example would be Debussy’s ‘Jeux’.
