Mixolydian

The Mixolydian Mode is the 5th mode of the major scale so it starts and ends on the 5th degree of that scale. If we play the notes of the C major scale but start on G we get the following intervals:
TONE, TONE, Semitone, TONE, TONE, Semitone, TONE.

This means G Mixolydian is: G, A, B, C, D, E, F, G.

The Mixolydian mode is often written as 1 2 3 4 5 6 ♭7 and is similar to a major scale but with a flatted 7th.

G Major: G, A, B, C, D, E, F♯, G
G Mixolydian: G, A, B, C, D, E, F, G

The Mixolydian mode has a major sound but with a bluesy quality because of the flatted 7th. As a result, it is found more in non-classical music including folk such as the traditional Irish song ‘She Moved Through the Fair’. Other classic song examples are ‘Sweet Home Alabama’ (Lynyrd Skynyrd), the ‘Hey Jude’ outro (The Beatles), Back in Black (AC/DC) and ‘Royals’ (Lorde).
